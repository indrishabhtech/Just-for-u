Why breathing helps 🌿
🧠
Reduces pain perceptionSlow deep breathing activates your parasympathetic nervous system — the "rest and digest" mode — which literally lowers how intensely your brain processes pain signals.
😌
Calms anxiety instantlyThe extended exhale triggers a vagal response that slows your heart rate and stops the cortisol spiral within minutes. It's faster than any medication.
🌡️
Relaxes cramped musclesAs your nervous system calms, blood flow to abdominal muscles increases. Combined with heat therapy, breathing exercises can cut cramp duration significantly.

Tips for your phase
What always helps 💛
🌡️
Heat therapy firstWarm water bottle on your lower belly for 20-minute intervals. Increases blood flow and relaxes uterine muscles — more effective than most painkillers alone.
🍫
Dark chocolate is genuinely good70%+ dark chocolate contains magnesium which directly relaxes muscles and reduces prostaglandin levels. You're not craving it randomly.
💧
Warm fluids, alwaysChamomile tea reduces prostaglandins (the compounds causing cramps). Ginger tea reduces nausea and bloating. Avoid cold drinks during cramps.
😴
Rest without guiltYour uterus is contracting as hard as early stages of labour right now. Rest is the right thing. Not a luxury — a requirement.
🛀
A warm bath resets everythingAdd epsom salt or a few drops of lavender oil. It relaxes every muscle group and quiets the nervous system more than almost anything else.
🧘
Gentle yoga helps more than lying stillChild's pose, cat-cow, supine twist — these stretch uterine muscles and increase blood flow. Even 10 minutes significantly reduces cramp intensity.
🌙
Sleep position mattersFoetal position (on your side, knees pulled up) reduces tension on abdominal muscles. A pillow between knees helps further.
💊
Ibuprofen works best earlyTake it (Combiflam / Brufen 400mg) at the very first sign of cramps — not when pain is severe. It's an anti-prostaglandin. Early = most effective.
📵
You don't owe anyone responsesCancelling plans, putting your phone down, needing silence — all completely valid. No explanation required. Anyone who matters will understand.
Did You Know? 🧬

🌊 Cramps happen because your uterus contracts to shed its lining. These contractions are driven by prostaglandins — the same compounds causing inflammation. That's why ibuprofen (anti-inflammatory) helps more than paracetamol.

🧬 The average person has about 450 menstrual cycles in their lifetime. Every single one is a sign of a healthy, working body — even when it doesn't feel that way.

🧠 During the luteal phase (pre-period), your brain's emotional processing centres are more active. You literally feel things more deeply. That's not weakness — that's your brain running on a different, more emotionally intelligent frequency.

🌱 The follicular phase (right after period ends) is when estrogen peaks. Most people feel their most creative, social, and energetic then. A good time to plan things you want to do.
